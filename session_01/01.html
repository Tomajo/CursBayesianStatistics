Statistics and “the two cultures”

The two cultures

Statistics (according to Breiman)

  • Focus on understanding (as opposed to predicting)
  • …
  • White box (generative)
  • But limited number of generative models

Traditional statistics

  • Focus on solutions, not problems (in particular, solutions requiring low computing power)
  • Weak link with decision theory (in particular, p-values, etc., i.e., low bandwidth outputs)

Statistics, systems, and models

What statistics is

Statistics is a technology concerned with stochastic data generating systems (SDGS) for the purpose of making informed decisions.

SDGS: Stochastic data generation systems

  • Systems that generate data with a structured random component

  • They happen mostly in the real world:

    • Outcomes of patients at hospitals
    • Summer (heat wawe related deaths)
    • Sales of products in supermarkets

SDGS and decisions

  • We are concerned with SDGS because we need to make decisions
  • In fact, informed decisions
  • Standard statistics provide weak material for informed decisions: mostly, the (highly controversial) p-value
  • Strong statement: decisions should be integral part of statistics

SDGM and SDGS

  • We cannot work with SDGSs directly: we need stochastic data generation models
  • SDGMs are conceptual approximations to SDGSs
  • There is a problematic link between both
  • Machine learning solves it with the (R)MSE:

\[\frac{1}{N}\sum_i \left(\hat y_{i} - y_i \right)\]

Statistics via generative models

Traditional statistics

  • Consists in a menu of solutions: t-test, lm, GLM, etc.

  • People tend to choose from this menu when facing a SDGM. E.g.:

    • People use the t-test because it is popular (and because of lack of alternatives)
    • They spend so much time justifying the use of the test (out of horror vacui)
    • The t-test becomes popular because people use it

Statistics based on generative models

  • Produce a SDGMs coherent with a given SDGS
  • Fit the model
  • Produce output useful for decisions

Note that the program above is only feasible today, not at the time of the founding fathers of statistics. In part thanks to tools such as Stan (and JAGS, BUGS,…).

Model fitting

  • SDGMs known up to some parameters
  • Very often, something is known about (some of) these parameters (something often disregarded!)
  • Fiting a model: reduce the uncertainty about these parameters using data

Plato’s cave

Is this program feasible?

  • It depends:

    • Very often, it is
    • Very often, it is impractical, though

  • In many cases, you need to rever to traditional methods. But not all is lost:

    • Often, it is possible to enrich our traditional approaches with ideas coming from generative models
    • New (traditional) models can be created that accelerate fittings using tricks in given contexts
    • People are working in parallelization for big problems and the results are promising
